# Future Services Positioning Doctrine

**Parent doctrine:** [`../website_master_messaging.md`](../website_master_messaging.md)  
**Role in hierarchy:** Master Messaging Doctrine -> Page-Level Messaging Doctrine -> Website Implementation  
**Scope:** Governance rules for future service development and positioning control.

---

## Page Purpose

Define how future services inherit and reinforce the clarity-first operational intelligence identity, while preventing service sprawl and positioning drift.

---

## Primary Audience

- Internal strategy and content teams
- Future collaborators editing service messaging
- AI assistants generating or revising service-page documentation

---

## Core Messaging Goal

Ensure every future service remains aligned with:

- Clarity before technology
- Operational intelligence positioning
- Diagnostic-before-prescription discipline

---

## Primary Emotional Outcome

Internal confidence that expansion can happen without strategic identity erosion.

---

## Primary Operational Outcome

New services are scoped, described, and launched using consistent messaging architecture tied to clear operational outcomes.

---

## Messaging Priorities

1. Keep category identity stable as service portfolio grows
2. Preserve diagnostic sequencing before implementation sequencing
3. Tie every service to concrete operational decision value
4. Avoid language that implies software-platform positioning
5. Maintain disciplined scope boundaries for each offer

---

## Approved Messaging Themes

- Diagnostic before prescription
- Operational intelligence before automation
- Capacity and decision clarity as service outcomes
- Structured engagement pathways
- Clarity-led prioritization

---

## Messaging To Avoid

- Service naming that mimics SaaS product tiers
- Broad, vague "transformation" claims
- Redundant services that differ only by wording
- Hype-first service launches disconnected from operational doctrine

---

## CTA Philosophy

Future service CTAs should follow pathway discipline:

1. Front-door qualification via Data Clarity Call
2. Diagnostic anchor via Utilization Gap Audit (or explicitly related diagnostic)
3. Downstream service CTA only when diagnostic context is clear

No CTA should bypass clarity-first framing in top-level messaging.

---

## Tone Rules

- Executive and operational
- Structured and sober
- Specific and measurable where possible
- Calm confidence over promotional urgency

---

## Structural Notes

For each future service doctrine, include:

1. Problem framing
2. Diagnostic lens
3. Decision-quality value
4. Scope boundaries and non-goals
5. CTA routing logic

Use this sequence consistently before any implementation copy is drafted.

---

## Future Expansion Notes

### Service admission criteria

A new service should only be introduced if it:

- Solves a distinct operational clarity problem
- Has clear diagnostic logic
- Supports leadership decision quality
- Does not duplicate existing offer pathways

### Anti-sprawl rules

- Limit near-identical services with different names
- Consolidate overlapping offers under one coherent narrative
- Keep flagship hierarchy intact (audit and clarity call anchors)

### Positioning drift prevention

- Review new service language against `website_master_messaging.md` before publication
- Reject copy that shifts identity toward software vendor, hype AI agency, or generic freelancer model
- Require explicit mention of diagnostic and operational outcomes
